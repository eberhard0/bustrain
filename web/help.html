What BusTrain does

BusTrain tells you, for wherever you want to go: the next bus and train that actually get there, which is faster and which is cheaper, the exact sign to look for on the front of the vehicle, which stop to get off at, and it notifies you before departure and before your stop. It's made for visitors who can't read kanji and don't know the stop names — you search for the place ("Umi Jigoku", "airport", "Kannawa"), and BusTrain picks the right stops for you.

📲 iPhone: enable notifications (one-time, 1 minute)

Apple only delivers web-app notifications when the app is installed on your Home Screen (iOS 16.4 or newer). Do this once:

  1. Open bustrain.iameberhard.com in Safari and tap the Share button ⬆︎ at the bottom of the screen.
  2. Scroll down the share sheet and tap Add to Home Screen, then Add.
  3. Close Safari and open BusTrain from the new icon on your Home Screen — this matters: notifications only work from the installed app.
  4. Set any reminder (tap a 🔔 bell next to a departure, or "I'm taking this bus/train" on a journey) and tap Allow when iOS asks about notifications.

That's it. From now on, departure reminders and get-off alerts arrive like any app's notifications — app closed, screen locked.

Tapped "Don't Allow" by mistake? Open iPhone Settings → Notifications → BusTrain and switch Allow Notifications on.
Android & desktop: no install needed — just tap Enable on the notification banner (or Allow when the browser asks). Installing from the browser menu ("Add to Home screen" / "Install app") gives you an app icon too.

🧭 Plan a trip (the tourist way)

  1. Open the Search tab. "From" is your location automatically.
  2. Type where you want to go in any spelling — "umi jigoku", "airport", "鉄輪", "Oita station". Pick from the suggestions (sights ♨️, stations 🚉, bus stops 🚏, hospitals 🏥, malls 🛍️…).
  3. Read the two cards — Bus and Train — and the verdict on top: "Take the train → arrive 17 min earlier, pay ¥140 more. Take the bus → save ¥140." Times include all the walking.

Each card tells you everything you need:

  • The map — the blue dot is you, 🚏 is where you board (dashed line = your walk), 🎯 is where you get off, 🏁 is your destination. Pinch to zoom out and see the whole trip. Tap "🗺 to the stop" for Google Maps walking directions.
  • 🪧 Board the bus signed 「大分駅前」 — match these characters against the sign on the front of the vehicle. If it doesn't match, it's not your ride.
  • ✓ Get off at … — the 24th stop, ~12:16 — the alighting stop, checked against today's actual timetable, then the final walk to your destination.
  • 🚶 leave in 6 min — when to start walking so you don't miss it.

🔔 Reminders & get-off alerts

  • Departure reminders: tap the 🔔 bell next to any departure (Home, or a stop's full timetable). You'll be notified 5–30 minutes before it leaves — choose the lead time in the You tab.
  • Get-off alerts: tapping "I'm taking this bus/train" arms one automatically. It fires ~3 minutes before your scheduled arrival and — if the app is open during the ride — when GPS sees you within 400 m (bus) / 800 m (train) of your stop: "Get off NOW: 大分 Ōita — this is your stop!"
  • No account needed for any of this. Logging in (You tab) additionally saves your trip history and shows how much money and time you've saved.
What works when the app is closed: scheduled departure and get-off reminders (via push). What needs the app on screen: the GPS-based "this is your stop" alert — phone browsers stop GPS in the background.

❓ Good to know

  • Scheduled times only. The official data has no live delay feed, so leave a margin for important trips.
  • Fares: bus fares are exact (from the operators' fare tables); train fares are close estimates marked with "~". Limited-express prices include the unreserved-seat surcharge.
  • Coverage: Beppu & Ōita city — 1,400+ bus stops (Ōita Bus, Ōita Kōtsū, Kamenoi Bus) and 22 JR stations. More regions are planned.
  • Open source: the whole app and its data pipeline are on GitHub — adapt it to your own city.
